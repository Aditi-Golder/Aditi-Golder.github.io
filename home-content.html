Biography

I was born in Gopalganj, Dhaka, Bangladesh. I am currently pursuing graduate studies in the Department of Computer Science at Wake Forest University, North Carolina, United States. I hold a BSc in Software Engineering from Daffodil International University and an MSc in Information Technology from Jahangirnagar University, Dhaka, Bangladesh.

My research interests lie at the intersection of computer vision, machine learning, and remote sensing. I always focuse on developing automated, efficient, and interpretable machine learning systems, with particular emphasis on graph theory and representation learning. My work applies these techniques to complex problems in computer vision, remote sensing, and generative AI, aiming to deliver innovative, real-world solutions through advanced computational methods. Ultimately, I strive to make machine learning models more resource-efficient, transparent, and trustworthy.

I am deeply committed to the values of diversity and inclusion, believing that a diverse and welcoming community fosters a richer environment for learning, collaboration, and innovation. I am also passionate about leveraging knowledge and scientific research for the betterment of society and the protection of our planet.

In my free time, I enjoy reading books, listening to music, traveling and exploring new places, and spending quality time with my family and friends.
