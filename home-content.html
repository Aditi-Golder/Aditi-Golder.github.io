Biography

I was born in Gopalganj, Dhaka, Bangladesh. I am currently a graduate student in the Department of Computer Science at Wake Forest University, Winston-Salem, NC, where I was awarded a full-ride scholarship through a Graduate Teaching Assistantship. In addition, I work as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Intelligent Remote Sensing in Conservation & Discovery Group (IRSC Lab) at Wake Forest University under the supervision of Dr. Paul Pauca.

Prior to joining Wake Forest University, I earned my Master’s degree in Information Technology from Jahangirnagar University, Savar, Bangladesh, under the supervision of Professor K M Akkas Ali. During this period, I collaborated remotely with Dr. U Zeyar Aung from Khalifa University, UAE, and Dr. Mohammad Ali Moni from The University of Queensland, Australia. I completed my Bachelor’s degree in Software Engineering at Daffodil International University, Dhaka, Bangladesh, under the supervision of Dr. Nusrat Jahan.

Beyond academics, I believe nature has a powerful healing influence. I enjoy traveling and spending my free time in natural settings. I also like reading books, listening to music, and experimenting with new cuisines.

Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of computer vision, machine learning, and remote sensing. I focus on developing automated, efficient, and interpretable machine learning systems, with particular emphasis on:

  • Graph theory and representation learning
  • Generative AI for data-efficient training
  • Model robustness, transparency, and trustworthy ML
  • Applications in remote sensing and visual understanding

Ultimately, I strive to make machine learning models more resource-efficient, transparent, and trustworthy, while solving real-world problems responsibly.
