CervixMed: Detecting Cervical Cancer from Combinational Data using a Hybrid Architecture

Debashis Gupta1, Aditi Golder1,2, Md. Mahfuzul Haque3, Mohammad Ali Moni4
Department of Computer Science, Wake Forest University, North Carolina, USA
Institute of Information Technology, Jahangirnagar University, Dhaka, Bangladesh
Department of Computer Science and Engineering, BAUST, Saidpur, Bangladesh
AI and Cyber Futures Institute, Charles Sturt University, Bathurst, NSW, Australia

Proceedings of the 2023 International Conference on Digital Image Computing: Techniques and Applications (DICTA 2023)

Paper Code

A lightweight CNN (CervixMed) + classical ML on tabular risk factors, fused in a simple hybrid pipeline.

Abstract

Cervical cancer remains one of the most common cancers among women and benefits greatly from early screening. We propose three contributions: (1) a Hybrid Architecture that jointly considers patient tabular risk factors and cell-image analysis, (2) CervixMed, a lightweight CNN for five-way cervical cell classification, and (3) a simple GUI to streamline real-world use.

For tabular screening, classical ML models (KNN, SVM, LR, RF) are trained after PCA-driven preprocessing. LR and SVM each achieve 99% accuracy and 100% sensitivity. For images (SIPaKMeD), our CervixMed model surpasses strong transfer-learning baselines (DenseNet121/169/201, ResNet152v2, VGG19), reaching 94% accuracy and 98% sensitivity across five cell types.

The hybrid pipeline fuses the best tabular and image predictors at inference time. Combinations with LR or SVM plus CervixMed reach 96.5% overall accuracy. We also provide a prototype GUI that handles cloud preprocessing, model inference, MongoDB-backed logging, and result review.

Method Overview

Dataset. SIPaKMeD provides 4,048 isolated single-cell images from Pap smears across five classes: Dyskeratotic, Koilocytotic, Metaplastic, Parabasal, and Superficial‑Intermediate. Tabular clinical data comprises 858 records, 36 attributes, ages 13–84. Splits follow 70/15/15 for train/val/test.

Preprocessing. Images are resized to 64×64 with augmentations (rotation, flips, crops, Gaussian noise, contrast). Tabular features undergo imputation (primarily mean or logical fill for STD indicators) and dimensionality reduction via PCA.

CervixMed (CNN). A compact stack of Conv–ReLU–MaxPool blocks with Dropout for regularization. After flattening, dense layers (4608→128→64) feed a 5‑way softmax head for cell type classification.

Training & Baselines

Classical ML (tabular). Logistic Regression, SVM, kNN, and Random Forest; LR and SVM deliver the strongest results (99% accuracy, 100% sensitivity).

Transfer Learning (images). DenseNet121/169/201, ResNet152v2, and VGG19 serve as baselines. CervixMed attains 94% accuracy and 98% sensitivity, outperforming these models on average.

Evaluation. We report accuracy, sensitivity (recall), specificity, and AUC, observing minimal overfitting and strong generalization on test images.

Hybrid Inference

Fusing Tabular + Image Predictions

We run the best tabular model (LR or SVM) and CervixMed in parallel and combine their outputs as the final decision. The top combinations—LR+CervixMed and SVM+CervixMed—achieve 96.5% accuracy. Example cases show consistent agreement between tabular screening (positive/negative) and image classification (cell type), enabling a clear final verdict.

  • Tabular screening (e.g., risk factors) → Positive; Image class → Dyskeratotic ⇒ Final: Positive.
  • Tabular screening → Negative; Image class → Parabasal (normal) ⇒ Final: Negative.

GUI Workflow

The GUI accepts both image and tabular inputs, performs cloud-based preprocessing, executes model inference (SVM/LR for tabular; CervixMed for images), and stores inputs and predictions in MongoDB for audit and continual improvement. Results are presented in a simple dashboard to support rapid screening.

Related Links

Prior work spans classical ML and modern DL for cervical cytology and colposcopy images, including CNNs with transfer learning and multi‑modal fusion. Our approach differs by explicitly combining strong tabular screening with a lightweight, task‑specific CNN for images, and exposing both via a practical GUI.

  • DL baselines: DenseNet, ResNet, VGG.
  • Colposcopy/cytology integration and multi‑modal screening.
